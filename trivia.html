Trivia

  1. Tarlac is the province in Central Luzon with the most diverse population. The province is home to four district groups: the Pampangos, Ilocanos, Pangasinenses, and Tagalogs.
  2. Tarlac is noted for its superb cuisine as well as its extensive sugar and rice crops. The fact that it is the melting pot of Central Luzon contributes to its excellent cooking.
  3. Half of the population speaks Ilocano, while 41% speaks Pampango. The Tagalog language is widely understood.
  4. Rice and sugarcane are the main crops. Corn and coconut are also important crops, as are vegetables like eggplant, garlic, and onion, and fruit trees like mango, banana, and calamansi.
  5. Tarlac has its own rice mills, corn mills, sawmills, and logging operations. There are three sugar centrals in this cake. Other businesses cater to agricultural requirements, such as fertilizer. Ceramics making has become important among its cottage industries as a result of the large supply of clay.
