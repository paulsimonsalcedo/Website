TOPONYMS

TARLAC

(Themeda arundinacea (Roxb.)) is a wild grass that grows in damp, sloping steep places selectively and abundantly. Tallak is the indigenous name for the reed, which is also known as tanglar among Ilocanos and malatarlak among Kapampangans. The word Tarlac is an orthographic phrase that was most likely coined by Spaniards. The Filipinos' early spelling utilizes k instead of c. The letter c has a Spanish origin.

Bamban

The weed Donax cannaeformis (Forst. F) is used to make baskets.

Capas

Capas (Ceiba pentandra) gets its name from bulak (Kapampangan) and kapok (Tagalog). Capas is a term used in the Ilocano language. Kapas-kapas, also known as pusa-pusa, is a vine that grows in the Philippines. Pinakbet is made with its flowers as a combination.

Conception

Concepcion was once a part of the Pampanga town of Magalang. It is a gorgeous green town with rice fields, forests, and rivers. The Calius tree (Calius lactescens Blco.) has fine branches that are used to build Christmas trees and decorations.

Anao

Anao (Livistona rotundifolia Lam.) is an indigenous name for a palm tree used to produce hats and rain capes. Anao is now well-known for its ilang-ilang (Cananga odorata Lamk.) production.

Gerona

The great egret (Egretta alba) gave Gerona its name. Previously, the town was known as barog (forest). Egrets, sometimes known as tagak, can still be seen in the town's environs.

La Paz

La Paz was once a part of Arayat, Pampanga. Cawayan (Bambusa blumeana Linn.) and Bayug were its first villages (Bambusa arundinacea Vill.).

Mayantoc

Mayantoc is derived from the word yantoc (rattan). Mayantoc is a term that refers to a large amount of rattan. Lawi is a broad name used by the Aytas to refer to several rattan species.

Moncada

Caarosipan (Antidesma bunius Linn.) was Moncada's previous name. Arosip is a dioecious tree that is tiny and smooth. It has a fleshy, crimson, sour yet edible fruit that is oval in shape and contains a single seed. Isip (Kapampangan), bignay (Tagalog), and ayhip are the names of the plant (Ayta). Caarosipan (barangay) connotes a lot of arosip. Due to a large influx of Ilocano migrants from Paoay, Ilocos Norte, Moncada was renamed Capaoayan. Moncada is said to have gotten its name from the word muskada.

Paniqui

Paniqui or pampaniki (Ilokano) denotes bat. During mango season, bats are thought to move from Zambales to Paniqui in quest of mango fruits. As a result, the town's name could have come from the short-nosed fruit bat (Cynopterus brachyotis) or the Philippine pygmy fruit bat (Cynopterus pygmaeus) (Haplonycteris fischeri).

Pura

Pura is said to have gotten its name from the town's all-Ilocano settlers. It's also possible that the town of Pura got its name from the purac plant. Pandan (Pandanus luzonensis Merr.) or screw pine is another name for purac or porac.

Ramos

The town of Ramos was previously known as bani (Pongamia pinnata Linn.), a tree species. It's a smooth tree that grows to be 8 to 25 meters tall.

Sta. Ignacia

The town of Sta. Ignacia was once known as Binaca (cow herd).

Victoria

The town of Victoria has a long history with (Barangay) Canarem (Diospyros lanceifolia Roxb.). The tree's grandeur is most likely why the Canarem River was named after it.
