Language

OLD KAPAMPANGAN WORDS

Langolango “toilet” Sipan “toothbrush” Amuyan “sponge” Lambangan “rice dispenser” Carangcarang “baby walker” Yubyub “barbeque” Limpasut “shark” Talangtalang “goldfish” Batanglaua “spider web” Lacsa “10,000” Gatus “100,000” Sangyuta “1,000,000” Payungdaguis “mushroom / mouse’s umbrella” Sulungdaguis “morning star / mouse’s lamp” Sabo susu “milk / soup from the breast” Bungang tudtud “fruit of sleep” Pinanari “rainbow / king’s loincloth” Anac sulip “illegitimate child / child from the basement”

a’bak (V.) mayabak, mayayabak, meyabak; to do something until morning; become morning.
(N.) morning.
a’be (V.) makiyabe ‘accompany’; mi-abeyabe ‘live together, stay together’,
(N.) friend, companion [used as a term of address], kayabe ‘companion’; pa:makiya’be ‘the skill of making and keeping friends.
ablas (V.) -an ‘to return something’; imitate. maki- -an ‘to answer back, retort’, mablas, minablas, paki-qablas, payablasan, inablas, ayablas, makayablas, meyablas, pamanablas, pekaqablas.
(N.) what one gives in return for something else. echo; revenge. kakablas ‘echo’.
agka’t (V.) summon, invite, signal. man-, -an, pa-, pan-.
(D.) may- ‘be invited’.
(N.) pa:managkat ‘manner of inviting’.
a’us (V.) call. ma:maus; awsan ‘to call someone’.
albe’ (V.) man-albe ‘watch’.
albe-n ‘watch something’; ayalbe.
albuga’n (N.) place where the sun sets.
(St.) mi-albug-an ‘be caught by nightfall’.
aldo’ (N.) sun; day. ganing-aldo ‘pre-dawn morning’, kayaldawanan ‘birthday; anniversary’.
(D.) aldo-aldo ‘daily’.
amba’g (N.) contribution. peka-ambag ‘thing given’.
(V.) [intransitive] man-ambag, pan-ambag, makapanambag. [transitive] i-ambag, ayambag, ayam-bagan, panambaga, ka-ambagan, pa-ambag, apayambagan.
ana’k (N.) child; (pl.) a:nak. anak-anakan ‘adopted child’; ina:yinak ‘godchildren’; pekayanak ‘substitute for a child’; anak-bastardo, anak-banggera, anak-puta, anak-lele-kwayan, anak-lalam-tete ‘illegitimate child’,
(V.) mang-anak ‘give birth’, maka-anak ‘capable of giving birth’.
(D.) kasinganak ‘the same age, age mate’.
a’pu (N.) grandparent, especially grandmother. As a term of address, apu is used for grandfather and grandmother; as a term of reference, usually it is restricted to grandmother, with ingkong being used for grandfather. With saints’ names, as: Apu Lusya ‘Saint Lucy’.
apu’ (N.) grandchild.
ba’bo (N., Prep.) up, upstairs, above.
(V.) mag-babo, magpababo ‘go up’; papababo ‘put something on top’; makapagpababo ‘able to go on top’.
(D.) mababo ‘on top, above’; maka-babo ‘leading (in election)’; maba’-bo-pa’liq ‘easily angered’.
bakba’k (N.) scar, scab, peeling skin.
(V.) mangabakbak, magpabakbak, mipamakbak ‘peel off something’.
(D.) mabakbak.
baga’su (N.) pulp, the part of the sugarcane which is discarded after milling; sawdust.
ba’ge (D.) matching, compatible.
(V.) bagay-an ‘match something to something else’.
(N.) thing, object, affair. bage-ba’ge ‘small things, details.
bale’ (N.) house. makibale’ ‘house owner, housewife’; kasi’ping-bale’ ‘neighbor’; balebalayan ‘play-house’.
kabu’d (Q.) unexpectedly, unannounced. kabudkabud; [syntactically comparable to agad].
kakla’k (D.) deaf. maklak, makaklak.
(V.) mangaklak, a:kaklak, apakaklak.
kutang (N.) question. kinutang ‘thing asked’; pa:mangutang ‘questioning, interrogative’.
(V.) mangutang, ikutang, a:kutnan, kitnan, kitnanan, kitang, pa:ngutnan-mangakutang, mikutang, mipangutang, makikutang, pakikutang, pa:kutang, a:kutang.
daka’l (N.) many, much. marakal, merakal, pekamarakal, kasing-dakal.
(V.) a:parakal, ka:rakal, pa:ngarakal, pa:rakalan, miparakal.
daki’tan (V.) fetch home, escort home; see dalakit.
dalu’mdum (N.) darkness.
(D.) madalumdum ‘dark, dim’.
(V.) pa:dalumduman.
da’nup (N.) hunger. makidanup ‘starvation’.
(V.) danupan, dumanup, pa:ranupan ‘make (pigs) hungry’, mangadanup.
(D.) madanup, medanup, pekamaranup.

(N.) gossip. endredista ‘a gossip’, kaindreduswan.
(D.) maindredus.
gabu’n (N.) earth; soil, dirt.
(V.) maggabun, makigabun, gabunan, ge:bunan.
(D.) magabun, megabun.

ga’lang (N.) respect, honor, politeness.
(D.) magalang ‘respectful, polite’.
(V.) galangan, ge:langan, igalang, pa:galang, a:galang, makigalang.
gala’ng (Adjunct) probability.

(V.) magpakabsiq, mangabsiq, mabsiq, pakakabsiq, pekakabsiq, kasingkabsiq.

(N.) pa:kalako ‘instrument to remove’.

nandi’n (N./Q.) a while ago.
nangnang (V.) roast, barbecue. inangnang, magnangnang, ningnang.
(N.) ningnang ‘barbecued food’.
